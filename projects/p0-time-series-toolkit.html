Cross-Domain Time Series Analysis Toolkit

A three-part exploration of how analytical methods from molecular simulations can be applied to complex time-series data — here using Bitcoin market data as a sandbox. Demonstrates signal processing, statistical inefficiency analysis, and committor-based regime detection.

Project Overview

In computational chemistry and materials science, we often face challenges in extracting meaningful information from noisy, correlated time-series data. This project adapts three proven techniques from my research — signal smoothing, statistical inefficiency quantification, and committor analysis — to cryptocurrency price data. The result is a versatile toolkit for detecting patterns, quantifying independent samples, and identifying transition regimes.

Featured Posts

Surfing the Spikes: Signal Processing for Crypto Peaks & Troughs

Using Savitzky–Golay filtering, slope, and curvature analysis to detect peaks and troughs in noisy BTC price data — methods equally applicable to MD trajectories or experimental sensor data.

Your 1,000 Price Bars Are Actually 12: Statistical Inefficiency in Crypto

Applying statistical inefficiency analysis from PyMBAR to quantify correlation lengths and effective sample sizes — ensuring robust, independent conclusions.

On the Knife Edge: Committor Functions for Market Transitions

Borrowing committor analysis from rare-event molecular simulations to identify unstable transition regions in BTC price dynamics.
